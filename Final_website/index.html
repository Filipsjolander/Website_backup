A classification of Glioblastoma

Welcome!

This is a research project with the aim of classifying different cases of Glioblastoma, a type of brain cancer. It is an unusually heterogenic type of cancer and shows up in many different forms. Our approach is to crowd source data about a collection of images, which we will feed to a model trying to assign coordinates fitting to the data. The images are photographs of brain slices infested with Glioblastoma, where the brains come from mice.

In each of the three comparison windows, we have presented a default view of the slice closest to the point of injection. However, do not forget to zoom and pan the image in order to get a clearer view of the type of growth in that particular brain.

Thank you for your participation!

Instructions:

  1. You will be provided three images of cases of Glioblastoma.
  2. Upon inspecting them, you will decide which case is the most unlike the others in terms of cancer growth. In practice, this will be the case exhibiting the most unique growth characteristics..
  3. You will then click on the corresponding button to select it.
  4. The process is done and will start over with three new images.
